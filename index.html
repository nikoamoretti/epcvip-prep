Technical knowledge

Ping-Post Mechanics

He built this system. Don't recite a textbook. Show you understand the flow conversationally.

Consumer fills a form on a landing page (EPCVIP-owned or affiliate-owned). Consent is captured via TrustedForm or similar.
PII gets stripped. A "ping" is created with only attributes: state, ZIP, credit range, loan amount, income, employment status.
Ping goes out simultaneously to all matching buyers. They evaluate against their campaign filters and bid — within milliseconds.
Platform decides: exclusive vs. shared. One winner, or multiple buyers if total shared revenue exceeds the exclusive bid.
Winner(s) receive the "post" — full PII plus consent certification.
If rejected → waterfalls to next highest bidder. Recovery revenue on a sunk traffic acquisition cost.
Key Metrics

EPC — The Name of the Company

EPC = Total Earnings ÷ Total Clicks. Example: $500 earned from 75 clicks = $6.67 EPC.

EPC is THE metric publishers use to decide which network to send traffic to. Higher EPC → publishers prefer your network → more supply → better leads → buyers bid more → higher EPC. That's the flywheel.

EPCVIP literally named the company after this metric. Their entire value proposition is delivering the highest EPCs in the industry.

How Buyers Actually Buy (What BizDev Negotiates)

The price is set by the auction, not by contract. BizDev and Client Relations negotiate the terms of participation:

SettingExample
VerticalPersonal loans, debt consolidation, auto finance
Geography38 states (lenders are licensed state-by-state)
Credit range620+ (don't send subprime leads)
Daily capMax 200/day — sales team can only handle so many calls
Ramp scheduleStart at 50/day, scale to 200 over 30-60 days
Max bid ceilingThe most a buyer will pay — auction can't exceed this

Campaign = a buying configuration. One buyer often runs multiple campaigns simultaneously. When you say "buyer volume drops" it's per-campaign, not per-buyer.

Publisher Quality Signals

Quality is measured by what happens to their leads after the auction: acceptance rate drops, return rate spikes, conversion rate falls, fill rate drops, bid prices fall, duplicate/fraud rate increases.

"The automation should trigger on buyer-side metrics — acceptance rate, return rate, conversion rate — because those are the leading indicators. By the time compliance catches something, the damage is already done. The alert should say: Publisher X's acceptance rate dropped from 68% to 41% this week in personal loans — investigate before buyers escalate."

Questions to Ask Valeriy

Your original 5 are strong. Add 1-2 from the research-backed list. Don't ask generic questions about culture or the team.

Your Original Questions (All Good)
Where's the biggest gap between where the revenue org is today and where you want it in 12 months?

Lets him define the vision. Shows you're thinking about his agenda, not just your role.

What made you decide to create this role now?

Tells you how urgent the pain is and what specifically triggered the decision.

How do you want this role to work with John's engineering team on the data side?

Shows you understand the data bridge requires cross-functional partnership.

What's one thing you wish the revenue teams had better visibility into today?

Gets him to define the pain in his own words. His answer is your roadmap.

What does winning look like for whoever takes this role in year one?

Clean, direct. Results-oriented. Shows you're thinking about deliverables, not activities.

Research-Backed Additions (Pick 1-2)
"You built the platform yourself from v1. How do you think about the balance between building more tech versus getting the existing data into the hands of the revenue teams?"

Speaks directly to his builder identity. Frames your role as the bridge. Also reveals how much engineering support you'd get.

"I know the team went through the HubSpot migration. What's the sentiment — are people bought in, or is there still friction?"

Shows you understand change management. Migration was done by an external agency ~1 year ago. There are likely adoption gaps.

"With the EWA expansion and new verticals, how are the revenue teams structured to handle that — same team across all verticals, or will there be specialization?"

Shows you know their growth direction and think about org design.

  • Don't ask generic questions — "what's the culture like" or "can you tell me about the team."
