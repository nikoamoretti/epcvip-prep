Operating system

EOS — Entrepreneurial Operating System

EPCVIP runs on EOS. You need to speak this language fluently.

What is EOS

A practical framework for running a business. Based on the book Traction by Gino Wickman. Gives companies a set of simple tools to get everyone aligned, accountable, and executing. EPCVIP uses it company-wide — quarterly townhalls, L10 meetings, scorecards, rocks.

Why it matters for you: your role is to install and run the EOS operating rhythm across Boris's 3 revenue teams. If you can speak EOS fluently, you signal you can hit the ground running.

The 6 key components
ComponentWhat it means
VisionEveryone aligned on where the company is going. Core values, 10-year target, 3-year picture, 1-year plan. At EPCVIP this comes from Valeriy.
PeopleRight people in the right seats. "Right people" = share core values. "Right seats" = they GWC the role (Get it, Want it, Capacity to do it).
DataRun on a handful of numbers, not feelings. Each person has a number they own. This is the Scorecard.
IssuesSurface issues honestly and solve them using IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve). No sweeping problems under the rug.
ProcessDocument and follow core processes. "The way we do things here." Consistency, not heroics.
TractionAccountability and discipline. Rocks (90-day priorities) + L10 meetings (weekly pulse).
L10 meeting — the weekly heartbeat

L10 = "Level 10" meeting. Same structure every week, 90 minutes max. Rated 1-10 at the end (aim for 10).

SegmentTimeWhat happens
Segue5 minGood news — personal and professional. Sets positive tone.
Scorecard5 minReview the numbers. Each metric is on/off track. No discussion — just flag what's off.

"One L10 per team per week. Scorecard data is automated before the meeting starts. Meeting is about solving problems, not reporting numbers."
Rocks — 90-day priorities

Rocks are the 3-7 most important things for the next 90 days. Named after the Stephen Covey "big rocks" analogy — put the big rocks in first, then the gravel fills around them.

  • Company sets 3-7 rocks per quarter
  • Each person owns 1-3 rocks
  • Rocks are specific and measurable — "Implement L10 across 3 revenue teams" not "improve operations"
  • Reviewed weekly in L10 (on/off track), evaluated at end of quarter (done/not done)
  • 80%+ completion rate is healthy

EPCVIP's Q3 2025 townhall: 229 goals achieved, 850 milestones. That's rocks + to-dos in action.

Scorecard — run on numbers

Each team and each person has 5-15 measurable numbers reviewed weekly. The point: spot problems early, before they become crises.

PrincipleMeaning
Activity ≠ resultsMeasure outputs (leads delivered, revenue retained) not inputs (hours worked, emails sent)
Leading indicatorsMeasure things that predict the future (pipeline, acceptance rate trending) not just report the past (revenue last month)
One ownerEvery number has exactly one person accountable. No shared ownership.
Green/redNumber is on track or off track. No yellows, no "kind of." Binary.

Your role: define the scorecards for all 3 teams and automate the data so it's accurate before the L10 starts. The meeting should never be spent arguing about whether the numbers are right.

IDS — how EOS solves problems
StepWhat
IdentifyState the real issue. Not the symptom — the root cause. "Publisher quality is dropping" → "We don't have automated quality alerts so we catch it too late."
DiscussOpen discussion — but focused. No tangents. No solving yet. Just understand the issue fully. Max 10-15 min.
SolveDecide on a solution, assign a to-do with an owner and a deadline. Done. Move to the next issue.

Most teams waste L10s discussing instead of solving. IDS forces resolution. Every issue ends with a to-do that has a name on it.

Technical knowledge

Ping-Post Mechanics

He built this system. Don't recite a textbook. Show you understand the flow conversationally.

Consumer fills a form on a landing page (EPCVIP-owned or affiliate-owned). Consent is captured via TrustedForm or similar.
PII gets stripped. A "ping" is created with only attributes: state, ZIP, credit range, loan amount, income, employment status.
Ping goes out simultaneously to all matching buyers. They evaluate against their campaign filters and bid — within milliseconds.
Platform decides: exclusive vs. shared. One winner, or multiple buyers if total shared revenue exceeds the exclusive bid.
Winner(s) receive the "post" — full PII plus consent certification.
If rejected → waterfalls to next highest bidder. Recovery revenue on a sunk traffic acquisition cost.

EPC — The Name of the Company

EPC = Total Earnings ÷ Total Clicks. Example: $500 earned from 75 clicks = $6.67 EPC.

EPC is THE metric publishers use to decide which network to send traffic to. Higher EPC → publishers prefer your network → more supply → better leads → buyers bid more → higher EPC. That's the flywheel.

EPCVIP literally named the company after this metric. Their entire value proposition is delivering the highest EPCs in the industry.

The price is set by the auction, not by contract. BizDev and Client Relations negotiate the terms of participation:

Campaign = a buying configuration. One buyer often runs multiple campaigns simultaneously. When you say "buyer volume drops" it's per-campaign, not per-buyer.
